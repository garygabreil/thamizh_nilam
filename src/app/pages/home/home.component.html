Our Story

"Thamizh Nilam Thamizh Pannai" is a food forest sprawling across 15 acres near Mamallapuram. This forest boasts over 16,000 trees representing more than 350 species. Planted 12 years ago, these trees have now reached impressive heights of up to 60 feet.

This forest is expertly crafted to merge agriculture with tourism, offering visitors an enriching, educational, and immersive experience that narrows the divide between urban and rural farming practices.

Meet the Visionary

This lush forest was brought to life by Mr. V. Deivasigamani, also affectionately known as Iraiazhagan, a 68-year-old Mechanical Engineer. Hailing from a traditional farming family, he has been engaged in farming since his childhood.

Inspired by his meeting with Nammazhvar, an esteemed Indian Green Crusader, he decided to devote himself entirely to farming, integrating agriculture with tourism to captivate visitors and spread awareness.

"Farming is not just a profession, it's a way of life that connects us to our roots and to nature."

Mr. V. Deivasigamani

Our Trees & Produce

Tree Varieties

Our forest has evolved to include a diverse array of timber trees, such as Vengai (Indian Kino), Marudham, Eeti (Rosewood), Redwood, Sandalwood, Kadambam, and teak, along with a selection of rare species like Purasu, Thadasu, Paathiri, Devatharu, Naruvizhi, Kusurukki, Kurinji, and Ichi (Indian Laurel Fig).

We focus exclusively on cultivating native and traditional seeds and crops, adhering to time-honored farming practices such as ploughing fields with bullocks and regularly rotating crops. Our farm is protected with a three-layer live fence composed of Timber-Bamboo-Timber.

Organic Practices

We are committed to sustainable agriculture by employing a range of organic practices on our farm.

Ploughing

Turning and aerating the soil, preparing it for planting to improve soil structure

Organic Inputs

Producing Panchakavya, Meen Amilam, and Jeevamirtham for natural fertilization

Seeding

Carefully selected native seeds sown for optimal growth and yield

Mulching

Covering soil with organic material to retain moisture and enrich soil
